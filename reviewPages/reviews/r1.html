Puridea Wireless Projector

Performs well, sleek and compact design - What more could you want?

Posted by Mohamed on May 16, 2016

I decided to unfollow the tradition review style with this one. I tested the projector over a week and made bullet points of my findings.

  • The projector comes with a very simple OS that should be easy to navigate for all.
  • Built in video player that allows multiple functions including play/pause, forward/rewind - it has also been compatible with all video files that I’ve tested: mkv and mp4 to name some.
  • There is an interface to update the software over the air via Wifi, this was the first thing I done after first boot - there was an update available.
  • Under settings, you are given the ability to adjust the projectors brightness - 2 options “high” and “low” and language.
  • OS contains clear instructions on how to connect to all compatible devices
  • Setting up Wifi on the projector was also very clear - the wifi setup is very similar to how the Google chromecast setups up a wifi connection.
  • Surprised that the projector was able to achieve over 100 inches at such a compact size - bare in mind that the room must be extremely dark at that size but it was still capable.
  • Not exactly a problem with the device but, the protocols used to connect to the device are wifi based. I attempted to connect to the projector using my PC with a wired connection but it did not pick the device up until I connected my PC via wifi.
  • I haven’t noticed any latency between devices but this can be completely dependant on your wireless speed/router.
  • After fully charging the battery - I was able to get a total of around 3.5 hours (4:23pm - 7:53pm) use from the device before it died. This included watching a movie and streaming my iPad for the remaining time.
  • Due to the size of my room, I mostly used the device at around 65 inches - I was able to view most images during the day (with curtains closed) but black images/scenes were hard to see.
  • The remote is very useful, it has quick access buttons so navigating between modes is a breeze.
  • The projector is advertised as 60” @ 1.66m, I was able to achieve 56.5” @ 1.52m (5ft) you are pretty much getting what was advertised.
  • There is an accessories box in the compartment below the projector - it comes with a white micro usb cable and a UK charing plug that emits a green LED when turned on. There is also the remote and tripod that extends up to 9”.
  • The built-in speaker is not too great, sounds muffled. An external speaker is a must if you’re watch/listening to media on the device. This is expected due to the small size of the device.

Overall, very impressive device for its compact and sleek design. It has professional packaging and includes all necessary equipment for use. I found the image quality to be very good while watching movies. I was able to watch a football match with my curtain partly opened - dark scenes are a little hard to see if the room is not completely dark.
